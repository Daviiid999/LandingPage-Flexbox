Some of our latest projects

Hence Flagship Store Madrid

The flagship Hence Store project in Madrid had the primary goal of modifying the layouts and uses of a 900 m2 space spread across three floors, with only 30 m2 at street level.

To achieve this, the decision was made to place the brand's offices on the top floor and consider the retail space as the first two floors, which are entirely visible to the customer from the entrance. All existing stairs were demolished, leaving a stairwell as a spatial connection between the offices and the store, where three large trees were positioned.

Edmmond Valencia

The Edmmond Valencia project is located in front of one of the city's most emblematic monuments, the Mercado de Col√≥n, and therefore, the store's facade reflects the elegance and sobriety of this location.

Inside the premises, a very limited palette of colors and materials is employed, conceptualizing the intervention with the use of white walls to enhance brightness, continuous micro-cement flooring to expand the space, and ash wood carpentry in decorative details to add warmth.

LDS Pharmacy

At Sud Pharmacy, we faced the challenge of addressing the issue of an old building constructed in multiple phases, upon which we needed to implement the program for a newly opening pharmacy.

The diverse structures erected in each phase, some made of concrete and others of steel, made it challenging to comprehend the overall extensive area available. After removing the most detrimental pillars for the space, we found the solution to this problem to be the guiding thread of the project: the metal joints of the nearest concrete and steel pillars would serve as the decorative focal point.

Muroexe Goya

Continuing with the aesthetic world created for the brand, we adapted the idea to a 100m2 space on one of the most emblematic streets in Madrid: Goya.

We chose to showcase the space in its raw state, with metallic pillars displaying fire protection and the structure with its ceramic vaults filled with the holes left by the passage of time. The current state is fixed by painting it white and leaving all the installations of the space and the building exposed.

Ritmo

The premises are located in the heart of the island and have the particularity of being a detached building, giving it magnificent presence from various perspectives.

The building rehabilitation project focused on removing palimpsests and emphasizing the semi-circular arches as the distinctive element of the structure. This intervention also aimed to repurpose the disused roof and integrate the building into the Mediterranean surroundings by applying a lime coating to the entire envelope.

EQA

Offices are not only workspaces; we must not forget the commercial focus that many of them have in their DNA. In this particular case, EQA is a company dedicated to the certification and verification of R&D&I, quality, and ASG standards; and that commercial aspect is part of its corporate culture. Therefore, the design of this office renovation project revolves around giving meaning and adapting the space to both commercial and purely work-related needs.

With over 600 m2 of space, EQA's offices in Plaza Castilla (Madrid) stand out for their sober, serious character, but without forgetting the modern touch so necessary in today's workspaces.
